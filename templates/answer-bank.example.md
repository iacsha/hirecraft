# Answer Bank

Your personal library of pre-written answers to recurring screening questions.
Copy, tweak per role, and paste. Never write the same answer twice from scratch.

Populate this with YOUR real answers. The examples below are prompts and structure
guides, not content to copy. Every answer should trace to something real.

---

## Profile / Background

**"Tell me about yourself."**
```
[2-3 sentences: what you do, how long, what makes you different.
No life story. Lead with your strongest credential for this role.]
```

**"Why are you leaving / why are you looking?"**
```
[Honest, forward-looking, no badmouthing. Frame around growth or
transition, not frustration.]
```

**"Why do you want to work here?"**
```
[Customize per company. Generic answers get filtered fast. Name one
specific thing about the company's product, mission, or team.]
```

## Compensation

**"What are your salary expectations?"**
```
[Single number, not a range. Add "open to total comp discussion."
Anchor at your target, not your floor. Example structure:
"I'm targeting $X, though I'm open to discussing total compensation."]
```

**"What are you currently making?"**
```
[In many states you are not required to disclose. You can say:
"I'd prefer to focus on what the role is budgeted for and whether
we're in the same range."]
```

---

## Work Authorization / Location

**"Are you authorized to work in [country]?"**
```
[Answer truthfully. Yes/No. Do not overcomplicate.]
```

**"Are you open to [on-site / hybrid / relocation]?"**
```
[Answer truthfully with your actual constraints. Vague answers
waste everyone's time and create problems later.]
```

## Technical Competency

**"Describe your experience with [key tool/skill from JD]."**
```
[Use honest framing:
- Production/hands-on: "I've used X in production for Y years, specifically..."
- Adjacent/familiar: "I haven't used X directly but have worked with [similar tool]..."
- Homelab/self-study: "I've worked with X in a lab/self-study context, not production."
Never claim production experience you don't have.]
```

**"How do you approach a complex integration / implementation?"**
```
[Walk through your actual process. Discovery, requirements, mapping,
testing, go-live, support. Use a real example if you can.]
```

## Questions to Ask Them

Always have 3-5 ready. Generic questions signal low interest.

```
- What does success look like in the first 90 days?
- What are the biggest challenges the team is facing right now?
- How does this team collaborate with [relevant adjacent team]?
- What does the on-call / escalation model look like?
- What's the biggest thing that would help this role succeed that
  you haven't been able to prioritize yet?
```

## Usage Notes

- This file is your DRAFT layer. Paste the right answer, then tweak
  for the specific company and role before submitting.
- Personalize the "Why here?" section for every single application.
  Recruiters can tell when it's generic.
- If a screening question asks something not covered here, add your
  answer after you write it. The bank grows with every application.
- Save company-specific tailored versions to the company folder, not here.
  This file stays reusable.
